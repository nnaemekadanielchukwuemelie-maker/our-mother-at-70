Victoria Offodimma Chukwuemelie

Born May 24, 1956  ·  Celebrating 70

“

A Life That Touched Generations

A Woman of
Extraordinary Love

Mum came into this world on the 24th of May, 1956, in the city of Port-Gentil, Gabon. Her own mother, our grandmother Comfort Orjioke, was flown to the hospital by helicopter to give birth to her, and days later Mum was carried home in a John Holt helicopter, the company our grandfather worked at then. From her very first breath she arrived in our family with a story you could not have written.

From her earliest years, Mum carried a quiet strength and a kind of grace that drew people to her. She did things right, she looked out for everyone behind her, and she carried herself with an authority that came not from demanding respect but from simply deserving it. She was, in every way, her father's daughter.

In 1980, Mum said yes to the marriage that would become the foundation of everything we know. Together with our father she raised ten of us, each one different, each one loved as though we were her only one. She taught us to pray before we could walk, she fed us with food and with scripture in equal measure, and she built a home that has remained, after all these years, the warmest place any of us has ever known.

Seventy years later, Mum is still the steady heart this family comes home to. Her grandchildren run to her exactly the way we once did. Her prayers still arrive at every place we go before we do. And every one of us, in our own way, carries a piece of who she is. This page is our way of saying thank you, of saying we see her, and of saying we love her beyond every word the language will let us borrow.

Victoria Offodimma Chukwuemelie

Through the Years

A Journey of
Beautiful Moments

1956

Born with a Pen in Her Hand

On the 24th of May, 1956, Mum was born at a hospital in Port-Gentil, Gabon. Our grandmother, Comfort Orjioke, was flown to the hospital by helicopter to put to birth, and Mum was later carried home in a John Holt helicopter, the company our grandfather worked at then.

Our grandfather, Papa, made the journey to that hospital carrying a pen. He opened Mum's tiny newborn hand, placed the pen inside it, and gently folded her fingers around it. Nobody could explain how a baby only hours old held on to it, but she did.

Papa named her Victoria and Ofodimma, one name meaning victory, the other meaning purity. Two names that were not wishes but prophecies, spoken over a life that had only just begun. From her very first breath, Mum already bore the mark of her father: his complexion, his face, his quiet insistence on doing things right and doing them well.

1960s, 70s

A Daughter Like Papa

Growing up, Mum was always two steps ahead. She did things right, she looked out for those behind her, especially her younger siblings, and she carried herself with a quiet authority that came not from demanding respect but from simply deserving it. She was, in every way, her father's daughter.

That pen Papa placed in Mum's hand as a newborn was not merely symbolic, it was a calling. She fell in love with education early, and that love would never leave her. She approached learning the way she approached life: with seriousness, with grace, and with the belief that to do a thing well was to honour the people who came before you.

1980

Marriage & The Covenant

In 1980, Mum said yes, and that yes became the foundation of everything we know. She entered into marriage the way she entered everything else, with her whole self. The covenant she and our father made that year was not simply between two people; it was the beginning of a family, a legacy, and a love story that would take decades to fully tell.

Those who saw Mum in those early years of marriage saw the same woman we have always known: steady, devoted, and completely true. She built a home that was not just a house but a sanctuary, a place where faith was the first language, where the table was always set, and where no one ever left feeling unloved.

1980s, 90s

Ten Gifts from Heaven

One by one, ten of us came into the world: Chibuzor, Ifeoma, Chukwuka, Ijeoma, Uchenna, Nnamdi, Chisom, Nkechi, Nnaemeka, and Chisimdi. Ten personalities, ten dreams, ten sets of needs, and not once did any of us feel like anything less than her only one.

Mum began to teach us to pray before we could pray on our own. She showed us God before the world had a chance to show us anything else. Her voice reading scripture in the early morning became one of the holiest sounds we would ever know. Her kitchen was never just about food, it was the place this family healed, reconnected, and always came home to.

Career

A Life in Education

The pen Papa placed in Mum's newborn hand made a promise, and she spent her career honouring it. Mum gave herself to education, not merely as a profession but as a vocation. She shaped young minds with the same intentionality and care she poured into her own children.

Colleagues and students alike saw a woman who showed up with excellence, who led by the same principle she lived by at home: do things right and do them well. Her name, Victoria, was never just a name. It was a prophecy she lived out in full, in every classroom, every corridor, and every life she touched.

2010s

The Season of Grandchildren

Then came the grandchildren, Shiloh, Blossom, Awesome, Paradise, Zoe, Chukwuekeka, Princess, Chukwuzikora, Angel, and Oluchi, and Mum's love, rather than diminishing with the years, simply multiplied. Each grandchild runs to her exactly the way we once did. That is not coincidence. That is character.

Mum makes every grandchild feel like the centre of the universe. She tells them stories. She prays over them by name. She welcomes their parents as her own. Her home remains what it has always been: the warmest, most welcoming place in the world, a place where you arrive burdened and leave held.

2026

Seventy Years of Glory

Seventy years. Seven decades of love that never ran out, faith that never wavered, and a spirit that never stopped giving. From a hospital in Gabon, where her own father pressed a pen into a newborn hand, to this very moment, surrounded by ten children, eleven grandchildren, in-laws, siblings, and a family that stands as living testimony to everything Mum has poured in.

Today we celebrate not just a birthday but a life. Mum, Victoria Offodimma Chukwuemelie, you are the finest thing this family has ever known. Happy 70th Birthday, Mummy. We love you beyond all words.
